01 — The Role

Wore every hat.
Then wrote the manual.

Acts2 Network is a ministry network with 125+ teams across the US and Asia, focused on reaching college students, youth, and local communities. In Taipei, I held two roles at once: design and growth lead for the whole team, and campus co-leader at Fu Jen University. My job was to build the visual presence and grow the community both digitally and in person.

The Reality

A 10-person cross-functional team, all with full-time roles, running events, mentoring students, and producing content simultaneously. No design department. No content team. No inherited playbook.

It felt like a startup as everyone wore many hats, nothing was defined in advance, and the work didn't stop at 5pm. What it required was figuring out what the community needed, building the system to deliver it, and doing it fast enough to matter.

Other hats worn

✦ Chef for 30  ·  ✦ Trip planner  ·  ✦ Ambience director  ·  ✦ Part-time childcare for 8

The Two Jobs

Design & Growth Strategist — owned the visual output: Instagram strategy, posters, event assets, and guiding teammates to design better. And on the ground: flyering, tabling, and meeting people to build community presence beyond the screen.

Campus Co-Leader, Journey at Fu Jen — co-led the campus chapter. Event planning and logistics, student mentorship, weekly small group facilitation, outreach trip coordination within Taiwan, and 5 talks across campus and community events.

02 — Campus Leadership

Co-leading a campus —
knowing your audience changes everything.

I moved from California to Taipei without speaking Mandarin, while raising a child. Fast adaptation wasn't optional — it came from treating every interaction as something worth paying attention to.

The Audience

Fu Jen students lean toward fun and connection — not lectures, not heavy programming. That distinction shapes everything: what you design, how you run outreach, what goes on a flyer. Getting it right meant adjusting, not just executing a playbook built for someone else.

The Work

I owned event planning and logistics — the structure that made everything possible. Co-leading in a second language, across a cultural gap, meant the work started before any event did. I also mentored 10 students directly, in English, across a language and cultural gap.

What I took away: how to read a room you didn't grow up in, navigate conflict across cultural norms, and make calls when there's no playbook.

03 — Design & Growth

Growing a community —
design, AI, and presence.

Design was one hat out of many. Most of my time went to campus leadership, events, mentorship, and outreach — design got what was left. That constraint changed how I worked.

How it worked
Perplexity became the creative collaborator I didn't have time to find. I wasn't outsourcing the thinking — I was accelerating it. AI got me to a clear creative direction in minutes, so my time in Canva was execution, not wandering.

That's the call you make when you actually know who you're designing for.
