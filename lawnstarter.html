01 — The System Problem

Friction at the front door.

The audit covered three sister brands — LawnStarter, Lawn Love, and Home Gnome — across SEM landing pages, geo-targeted City Pages, and core conversion flows. All three shared the same root problem: the acquisition surfaces had been built for traffic volume, not for the psychology of a user deciding whether to trust a new service with their home.

"There was no dedicated product designer on growth. The funnel had been optimized for traffic — not for the people arriving in it."

01
Trustworthiness Initiative

Trust signals buried, skepticism high

Critical guarantees, insurance, and bonded-provider information were hidden — especially on mobile. Hotjar scroll depth data confirmed users were abandoning before reaching the sections that would have converted their skepticism. The signals existed; they just weren't where decisions were being made.

02
Information Initiative — Hotjar Validated

Phone number required before showing price

Users clicked "See My Price" expecting a price. Instead, they were asked for their name and phone number with no explanation of why. Session recordings showed visible hesitation at the phone number field — a trust breakdown at the exact moment of conversion intent. A classic bait-and-switch violation of user expectations.

03
Information Initiative

No ability to edit address — control removed

After submitting an address, users had no way to go back and change it. Reloading the page showed the same locked state. This violated User Control & Freedom and Error Recovery — two core heuristics — and blocked the "test before trust" behavior common among hesitant first-time users.

04
Conversion Epic

High-intent users, no persistent CTA on mobile

City Pages were dense with content but had no sticky CTA on mobile scroll. Users with genuine purchase intent had to scroll back to the top to act — a friction point that didn't exist on competitor surfaces. The fix was low effort and cross-applicable to all three brands.

02 — The Process

90 days of diagnostic rigor.

I bypassed the typical "getting to know you" ramp by treating the first three months as a structured diagnostic sprint across all three brands simultaneously. The goal wasn't to redesign anything yet — it was to build an evidence-based map of every place the system was failing users, organized into three named initiatives — Information, Trustworthiness, and a Conversion Epic — and stack-ranked by impact and effort.

Weeks 1–3

Heuristic audit — three brands, full funnel

Applied NNg's 10 Usability Heuristics across every acquisition surface on LawnStarter, Lawn Love, and Home Gnome: SEM landing pages, City Pages, and core conversion flows. Each violation was documented, categorized by heuristic, and rated by severity and effort — building the raw material for the final roadmap.

Weeks 4–6

Behavioral data grounding

Evaluated Hotjar heatmaps and session recordings to validate and pressure-test the heuristic findings. Rage-click patterns confirmed the logo navigation issue. Scroll depth data quantified where mobile users stopped engaging before trust signals appeared.

Weeks 7–10

Synthesis and prioritization

Mapped 44+ identified opportunities across an effort/impact matrix. Separated Quick Wins (implementable without engineering dependency) from Larger Rocks (requiring cross-functional resourcing). Aligned the roadmap with SEM team priorities to ensure design work directly supported paid acquisition goals.

Week 12

Strategic handoff

Delivered a complete, implementation-ready roadmap to Product and Engineering: prioritized backlog, annotated designs for Quick Wins, and a phased architecture plan for the larger conversion surface redesigns.

02b — Key Findings

What the audit surfaced.

Of the 44+ opportunities identified, two heuristic violations were the most structurally damaging — both traceable to specific user behaviors in Hotjar recordings, both fixable without full rebuilds.

Research Artifact — Hotjar Behavioral Data
Scroll depth & heatmap showing mobile drop-off before trust signals — LawnStarter City Page
Heuristic Violation — User Control & Freedom + Error Recovery

Users couldn't go back or correct mistakes

After submitting an address, there was no back button and no way to edit the input — even on page reload. Users who made a typo, or who wanted to check a different address before committing personal information, hit a dead end. The heuristic violation was explicit: the system removed user agency at exactly the moment it was needed most.

Heuristic Violation — Aesthetic & Minimalist Design + Visibility of System Status

Pages were dense; users couldn't orient themselves

City Pages violated Aesthetic & Minimalist Design — sections like "World-Class Customer Support" and vague high-tech copy added noise without value. On mobile, the lack of a persistent CTA meant users who had scrolled past the fold lost their sense of "what to do next." Both violations compounded to increase abandonment from high-intent visitors.

03 — The Work

High-leverage bets,
not surface polish.

Given the absence of a dedicated design resource before my arrival, the strategy wasn't to redesign everything — it was to find the smallest interventions with the largest measurable impact and use them to prove design's value to a team that hadn't had reason to expect it.

Conversion Epic — High / Low Effort

Sticky CTA on mobile scroll

Implemented a persistent CTA bar for all three brands on mobile — ensuring the primary conversion action was always accessible regardless of scroll position. Cross-applicable across LawnStarter, Lawn Love, and Home Gnome without requiring separate engineering efforts for each.

Impact: Conversion action always in reach — zero scroll-back required
Trustworthiness Initiative — High / Low Effort

Trust badging at the decision point

Applied Lawn Love's trust badge system to LawnStarter — surfacing guarantees, bonded-provider status, and insurance signals at the moment users were deciding whether to give personal information. Moved these from footer copy to prominent positions within the conversion flow.

Impact: Reduced skepticism at peak drop-off — no product changes required
Information Initiative — High / Low Effort

Phone number field — justify or restructure

Hotjar recordings showed users visibly hesitating before entering their phone number to see a price — with no explanation of why it was needed. Recommended either removing the requirement or providing explicit context inline. Longer-term: restructure the flow so users see a price before being asked for personal details.

Impact: Addressed bait-and-switch anxiety at highest-intent moment
04 — Cross-Functional Leadership

Operating inside the Iron Triangle.

Growth design at LawnStarter meant sitting at the intersection of three teams with different success metrics and different timelines. I operated as the connective tissue — translating between technical feasibility, marketing performance, and product strategy.

Product

Backlog architecture

Translated 44+ audit findings into a structured, effort-ranked backlog that Product could absorb into sprint planning — not a wish list, but an implementation-ready prioritization framework.

Engineering

Feasibility-first scoping

With no dedicated dev resource available for growth work, I scoped Quick Wins specifically for low-engineering-lift execution — changes deployable via CSS or content updates — to keep velocity high while Larger Rocks were resourced.

Marketing / SEM

Protecting paid acquisition ROI

Partnered directly with the SEM team to audit landing pages against their campaign targeting. Identified where design decisions (broken navigation, hidden offers) were silently eroding the performance of every paid click.
