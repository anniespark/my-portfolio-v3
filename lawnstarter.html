01 — The System Problem

Friction at the front door.

The audit covered three sister brands — LawnStarter, Lawn Love, and Home Gnome — across SEM landing pages, geo-targeted City Pages, and core conversion flows. All three shared the same root problem: the acquisition surfaces had been built for traffic volume, not for the psychology of a user deciding whether to trust a new service with their home.

"Every paid click carried a $19 offer. Clicking the company logo silently stripped it. Marketing had no idea."

01
Systemic Logic Failure — SEM Funnel

The $19 offer that disappeared at the logo

Clicking the company logo on an SEM page navigated users out of their promotional context, silently removing the $19 offer they'd arrived to claim. Marketing was spending on clicks the product's own navigation was converting to zero-dollar sessions.

02
Psychological Friction — Hotjar Validated

Users asked for trust before they'd received any value

Hotjar recordings showed consistent hesitation at the phone number field: pause, re-read, abandon. Users clicked "See My Price" expecting a price — they got an identity form with no explanation of why.

03
Heuristic Violation — User Control & Freedom

The address dead-end: no back, no edit, no recovery

After entering an address, there was no way to go back or correct a typo. Skeptical first-time users commonly want to test a service before committing personal data — the system gave them no path to do that.

04
Edge-Case Architecture — High-Intent Drop-Off

New builds, new developments: invisible to the database

Users with new builds couldn't proceed — the database couldn't match their address and there was no fallback. High-intent users the system had no way to recognize, and nowhere to send.

02 — The Process

90 days of diagnostic rigor.

The approach was evidence-first: heuristic analysis structured the audit, behavioral data validated it, and SEM funnel data revealed the systemic failures no single-surface review would have caught.

03 — The Work

High-leverage bets,
not surface polish.

No dedicated designer on growth before this. The strategy was to find the smallest interventions with the largest acquisition impact — and make each one self-evidently actionable.

Systemic Logic — Quick Win

Lock SEM navigation — protect the offer

Clicking the logo stripped the $19 offer context users had arrived on. Locking SEM page navigation was a low-lift product fix with direct Marketing ROI — not a campaign problem, a product problem.

Impact: Protected SEM offer integrity — design fix, not a campaign fix
Trustworthiness Initiative — Quick Win

Surface what earns trust; remove what doesn't

Moved guarantees and bonded & insured status to directly below social proof on mobile. Flagged "As Seen In..." sections without source links — removing low-legitimacy signals proved as important as surfacing high-legitimacy ones.

Impact: Trust architecture rebuilt at the decision point — signal elevated, noise cut
Information Initiative — Architectural Redesign

Price first, then ask for trust

Near-term: add inline context to the phone number field explaining why it's needed. Long-term: re-sequence the funnel so users see a price before giving anything — converting the form from a gate into a natural next step.

Impact: Reframed the entry flow around transparency, not data extraction
Edge-Case Engineering — Larger Rock

An alternative path for unrecognized homes

A modal triggered when address lookup fails, letting users enter property details freeform. Built for high-intent users with new builds who were hitting a dead end — customers the automated system had no way to recognize.

Impact: Recovered high-intent users the database couldn't recognize
